Natural Gas Analysis & JPMorgan Chase Simulation
📊 Natural Gas Analysis

In this project, I explored natural gas price data to better understand market patterns and trends. The work included:

Looking at how prices moved over time.

Using charts and visualizations to spot changes and cycles.

Practicing data cleaning and organizing messy real-world data.

The goal wasn’t just technical — it was about telling a clear story from raw numbers and learning how energy markets behave in the real world.

💼 JPMorgan Chase Job Simulation

This part of the work came from a virtual experience program where I stepped into the role of a quantitative analyst at JPMorgan Chase. The tasks involved:

Organizing and analyzing financial data.

Turning complex information into simple insights for decision-making.

Practicing professional skills that connect data science to business needs.

It was a hands-on way to see how large financial firms use data every day, and it gave me a taste of what working in a quantitative role might look like.

🚀 Why These Projects Matter

Both projects helped me grow in different ways:

The Natural Gas project sharpened my ability to handle real-world data and explain trends simply.

The JPMorgan Chase simulation connected my data skills with business thinking, showing me how to apply what I learn in class to real financial problems.

Together, they highlight my interest in data science, energy, and finance — and my drive to learn by doing.
